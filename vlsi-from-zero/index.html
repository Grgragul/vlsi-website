What is VLSI?

VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) is the process of designing millions (or billions) of transistors on a single chip. These chips power everything from mobile phones and laptops to cars and satellites.

VLSI combines concepts from electronics, digital logic, computer architecture, and programming. Verilog is used to describe how hardware should behave.

Who is this website for?

  • ECE students new to VLSI
  • Students confused about tools and design flow
  • Beginners learning Verilog practically
  • Anyone without access to paid EDA tools

What will you learn here?

  • Verilog basics and RTL design
  • Simulation and waveform debugging
  • Synthesis and netlist generation
  • How real ASIC-style VLSI flow works

How Learning is Structured

Content on this website follows a logical order:

  • Understand fundamentals first
  • Write small, clear Verilog modules
  • Simulate and debug step by step
  • Gradually move towards synthesis concepts

You are encouraged to learn slowly and correctly.

Frontend & Backend – A Quick Preview

VLSI broadly has two sides:

  • Frontend: RTL design, Verilog, simulation, verification
  • Backend: Timing, layout, physical design

Beginners should focus on frontend fundamentals first. Backend concepts are easier once logic understanding is strong.
